Abstract

Parkour is a grand challenge for legged locomotion, even for quadruped robots, requiring active perception and various maneuvers to overcome multiple challenging obstacles. Existing methods for humanoid locomotion either optimize robot trajectory for a single parkour track or train a reinforcement learning policy to walk with a significant amount of motion reference, not to mention implementing a general pipeline that performs parkour skills. In this work, we propose a framework for learning an end-to-end vision-based whole-body-control parkour policy that overcomes multiple parkour skills without any motion prior. We show that using fractal noise terrain can still train a zero-shot sim-to-real policy for humanoid robots. The trained parkour policy can jump on a high platform, leap over hurdles, long gaps, and much more. We demonstrate that this unified framework trains an end-to-end policy that can autonomously select parkour skills at the speed of 1.0m/s and can be easily transferred to humanoid mobile manipulation tasks.
